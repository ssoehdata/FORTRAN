Consider a circle of radius 1.0 situated within a square with sides of length 2.0.  Now consider only the upper right quadrant of the square.  The ratio of the circle area to the square area is area_circle/area_square = pi*r^4/1.  With a radius of 1, and solving for Pi, we find pi=4.0*area_circle/area_square.  So, use a Monte Carlo technique to estimate pi.  In particular, using a random number generator (see Lecture 13) generate values for x and y.  As returned from the random number generator, all of these values will sit somewhere within the square; some will also sit within the area of the circle.  Perform this x,y query a large number of times and take the ratio of the number of queries that are inside the circle to the total number of queries.  This will approximate the ratio of area_circle/area_square.  Hence you will have your estimate for pi.
Questions for this assignment

Using 10, 100, 1000, 10,000, and 100,000 total queries, compute an estimate for pi.  Compare your results.  Is this an efficient means of computing pi?
