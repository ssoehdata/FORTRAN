These examples illustrate addressing round-off errors using Gauss-Jordan elimination
in large systems of simultaneous linear equations. 

The sample data sets cover both well-conditioned, and ill-conditioned systems (those that exhibit round-off error).
Double precision is used to help reduce round-off errors, and can be used to produce correct answers
for even difficult systems of equations. 

The subroutine simul.f90 is from Ch.9 (example simul2 in that chapter) and
is used for comparison with the dsimul.f90 program from Ch11.

The driver program for testing is the test_dsimul.f90 program.

Three data sets are used SYS6.ILL, SYS6.WEL, and SYS6.SNG (this final set has no unique solution).

Note that these test sets are 6x6 arrays, although the subroutine 
makes use of allocatable arrays, and therefore unlimited array sizes 
are permitted. 

--------------------
Significant differences in the results for the single and double precision calculations on the ill-conditioned data set.

Essentially identical results are obtained for the single and double precision calculations on the well-conditioned
system. 

The subroutine (dsimul) correctly identifies and flags the lack of a unique solution for 
the SYS6.SNG set.
